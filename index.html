Dhwani: AI-Based Wildlife Monitoring and Threat Detection System

Automated sound-based monitoring system for detecting illegal activities such as tree cutting, poaching, and unauthorized human intrusion in wildlife environments.

Problem Statement

Illegal activities such as tree cutting, poaching, and unauthorized human intrusion pose significant threats to wildlife ecosystems. Traditional monitoring systems rely on manual surveillance, which is inefficient in large forest areas. An automated sound-based monitoring system is needed to detect suspicious activities in real time.

Literature Review / Market Research

Recent studies in environmental sound classification use deep learning techniques such as Convolutional Neural Networks (CNNs) to analyze audio signals. Acoustic monitoring systems have been applied in wildlife conservation and smart surveillance. However, many systems are complex or difficult to deploy on low-power devices.

Research Gap / Innovation

The proposed system introduces a single deep learning model capable of identifying both biological sounds and potential threat sounds in real time. The model is optimized using TensorFlow Lite for deployment on low-power devices such as Raspberry Pi.

System Methodology

Dataset / Input

ESC-50 dataset and custom environmental audio samples processed into Mel spectrogram features.

Model / Architecture

Convolutional Neural Network (CNN) model converted to TensorFlow Lite for real-time sound classification on Raspberry Pi.
